Welcome to the Second Annual Friends Maker Day!

Feb. 24, 2018 at school from 9:30 AM to 3 PM.

Maker Day is a celebration of creating, tinkering and building expressed as a series of parent-led workshops and drop-in activities. You can view photos of last year’s event here. This year we aspire to expand to include all Lower and Middle School grades, provide financial aid and simplify registration.

How to attend

There are two ways for students to attend: through workshops and drop-in activities. Workshops will run on a schedule and last up to 90 minutes each. You can sign up for a maximum of two separate workshops. See below for a comprehensive list of all workshops offered. Once you've made your decisions, you can register here. Please arrive on time for your workshops. For the drop-in activites no signup is required — come whenever it is convenient. If you sign up for a workshop, the drop-in activities are free, if not they are $20 which you can pay on Maker Day. Note, the drop in fee only allows access to the drop in activities and does not include lunch or snacks.

Workshops

Squishy Circuits

Leader | Meredith Barnett, Carrie Packard
Grades | K-2
Requirements | parental supervision for K-2
We will construct simple electrical circuits with pre-made conductive and resistant playdough. Jazz it up by inserting LEDs and connecting a battery, and presto…you have a playdough creature with light up eyes.

Felting

Leaders | Beth Bugdaycay, April Cantor and Margee Challa
Grades | K-3
Requirements | none
Learn how to paint with yarn by felting. You can add color and design to anything made of wool within minutes. Start learning by felting directly onto a pencil case that you will be able to complete and take home by the end of the session. All creative skill levels can produce something beautiful.

LED Cards

Leaders | Erika Bach and Partha Deb
Grades | K-4
Requirements | none
Have you ever received one of those really cool greeting cards that lights up? This is your chance to make your own. And if you’re feeling up to the challenge, use cardboard to create your very own version of your favorite school mascot! You’ll use a whole host of cool materials such as LEDs, paper clips and fasteners...just to name a few.

Visible Mending

Leader | Kate Sekules
Grades | 3-8
Requirements | bring garment(s) with hole(s)!
Got Holes? Let's make your old clothes into a work of art you can wear! A Visible Mend is a repair that shows and shows you care about our world. Have fun with sewing, and end up with a new favorite garment.

Programming micro:bit

Leaders | Jawahar Chirimar and Evan Haymes
Grades | 4-8
Requirements | basic Scratch programming
Apply your Scratch coding skills to program the micro:bit, a single board computer that features an LED matrix, motion sensor and more. Example programs include a digital Magic 8 Ball and networked Rock, Paper, Scissors.

Ring Making and Dog Tag Stamping

Leader | Yuval Alexander
Grades | 5-8
Requirements | none
Are you into jewelry? Dog tags, rings, bracelets, necklaces..any sort of bling? This is your chance to design and fabricate your very own sterling silver ring band, or to stamp your own design (names, initials) on a dog tag that you can wear with a ball chain necklace. You will use real jewelry-making tools like rubber mallets and hammers for stamping your project.

Japanese Woodblock Printing

Leaders | Nandini Chirimar, Gita Patel, Susan Rha, and Shumita Bose
Grades | 5-8
Requirements | none
Learn traditional Japanese Woodblock carving and printing. Participants will create their design on a 6”x8” piece of shina plywood using beautiful Japanese tools to carve their artwork. Next, using sumi ink, watercolors and a bamboo baren, they will print their carved blocks on paper to create their own pieces of art. The blocks can be reused at home to make more artwork, stationery, etc.

Simon Says Solder

Leaders | Jon Levine and Jan-Willem van den Dorpel
Grades | 5-8
Requirements | none
Solder your own “Simon Says” memory game…no soldering experience required. Your finished project should provide hours of fun for years to come.

BAaT: Battery-powered Arduino Alarm Technology

Leader | Paul Glimcher
Grades | 6-8
Requirements | none
Learn the basics of Arduino-based circuit design and coding. Arduino is a versatile microcomputer that can be used for anything from turning on the lights to powering video games. We will use an ultrasonic circuit for measuring distance, the same way a bat does. We will use the echo-distances to control a beeping circuit so that the sound our Arduino produces indicates distance to a "target". Next, we will use a small vibration circuit and motor as an alarm that will start vibrating when the "target" gets too close. Finally, we will customize the system so that anything we can think of that involves distance, sound and vibration can become a part of our BAaT.

Drop-In Activities

Cardboard Challenge

Leader | David Morrison
Requirements | hot glue: K-2 should be supervised
Somewhere between real life Lego and Minecraft, there is Cardboard Challenge: building structures with cardboard and hot glue.

Wobblebot Mini-Golf

Leaders | Suzannah and Karl Kellner
Optional | bring in old battery powered toothbrushes
Requirements | hot glue: K-2 should be supervised
Wobblebots, art bots, wigglebots! We will disassemble a battery powered toothbrush and use its motor to make a creature that moves, or a creature who draws for you. Tweak your mobile creature to navigate an obstacle course, or contribute to our art bot display project.

Bridge Building

Leaders | Johnathan Sandler and Drew Lang
We will collaborate to construct large bridges from toothpicks, popsicle sticks, gumdrops, tape and glue.

Name This Derby

Leader | Jeff Willoughby
Requirements | hot glue: K-2 should be supervised
Build and race your own contraption on our brand new original design Pinewood Derby-style raceway. And help us pick a better name.
